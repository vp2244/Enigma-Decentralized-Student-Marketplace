Enigma is a decentralized peer-to-peer marketplace for students, realized as four cooperating Ethereum smart contracts and a single dual-network web client. Participants transact in EnigCredit (ENGC), an ERC-20 credit token; item listings are recorded on-chain; payments are held in escrow until the buyer confirms delivery; and completed trades yield tamper-evident seller reputation. We describe the system's modular ``vertical slice'' architecture, the security properties enforced by each contract (owner-gated issuance, a checks-effects-interactions escrow, and one-rating-per-sale reputation), and an evaluation on a local Foundry/Anvil chain and the public Sepolia testnet. The token slice passes its full unit-test suite and was validated end-to-end through the hosted web client on both networks.
